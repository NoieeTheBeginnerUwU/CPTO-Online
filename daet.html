Daet

Daet is the capital town, the service center, the commercial hub, and the jump-off point of tourism attractions in the province of Camarines Norte. It takes pride as the home of National Hero Dr. Jose P. Rizal, locally known as "Bantayog". It is one of the popular surfing destinations in the country and is dubbed the "Surfing Capital in Bicol" with the presence of Bagasbas Beach, which is characterized by long and wide stretch of fine gray sand and waves that roll from Pacific Ocean, making it an ideal spot for surfers of any level. It is famed for the bountiful harvest if "Queen Formosa" the sweetest pineapple variety in the world from which "Pinyasan", the longest running festival in the province, is centered. #DAET #CapitalTownOfCamNorte #HomeOfQueenFormosa #TheSweetestPineapple

Daet, Camarines Norte, Philippines

Daet is a first class municipality and the capital city of Camarines Norte in Philippines. Daet is home to the monument of the first Filipino hero Dr. Jose Rizal. The Zip code of Daet is 4600. Daet’s economy is largely dependent to mining and tourism houses the reserves of diatomaceous earth or white clay. Education industry is also well supporting the economy of Daet and the best educational institutions include Camarines Norte State College, Our Lady of Lourdes College Foundation and Daet Elementary School.

Culture, Food and Shopping in Daet:

Daet has the inhabitants of greater percentage of Christians. The natives celebrate Christmas with great enthusiasm by gathering together. Daet houses numerous historical buildings, churches and cultural centres. Tagalog, Ilocano, Kapampangan, Bicol, and Pangasinense generally spoken languages in the areas of Daet and also English is used for business purpose. Daet is home to many food corporations, traditional food corners and modern restaurants with foods from China, Japan, India, Mexico, Italy, Thailand and Korea. The prices of the foods are similar to other regions of Daet. Daet’s carpets and embroideries are very famous and the products are available at all the handicraft shops of Daet. Also Daet hosts many modern shopping centres.

How to reach Daet?

Daet is well connected with major cities of the province and its surrounding regions through Maharlika Highway. The airport of Daet is situated 13 kms away from the midpoint of the town. There is one international seaport at Barangay Osmena in Jose Panganiban town serving all the major towns of the province including Daet and it one hour ride from Daet. Buses, Jeepneys, Motorcycles and Tricycles are very useful for local travel.
